Anthropic in talks for $30B raise at $900B valuation, would eclipse OpenAI for the first time

Bloomberg first reported on May 12 that Anthropic is negotiating a round led by Dragoneer, Greenoaks, Sequoia and Altimeter that would nearly triple its February $380B mark. As of today the round is still unsigned and is reportedly targeted to close by end of month.

▶

The figure that matters is not the headline valuation but the revenue trajectory underneath it. Anthropic has publicly said it crossed $30B in annualised revenue earlier this month, up from roughly $9B at the end of 2025. Bloomberg, FT (via secondary reporting) and Yahoo Finance all converge on the same set of lead investors and the same expected close window.

I want to flag one number that is widely repeated but worth pushing back on: a $44B Q1 ARR figure attributed to Anthropic appears in the BuildFastWithAI roundup. I cannot independently confirm that against Anthropic's own statements (the company has stated it crossed $30B), so I would treat $30B as the verified floor and $44B as unconfirmed.

The economics question still hangs over this: Anthropic's growth is token based, and the Ramp data in story 03 shows enterprise usage outpacing pricing power. A $900B mark prices in either substantially higher per token capture, or the conversion of inference workloads into much higher margin products. Both paths require compute Anthropic does not own.

Why It Matters for CoreWeave

Anthropic at $900B is the clearest signal yet that the buy side believes frontier lab spend is durable and accelerating. Every dollar of that valuation is implicitly underwriting GPU capacity Anthropic does not produce. CoreWeave is one of a small handful of specialised neoclouds that can absorb that demand at scale; the recent SpaceX Colossus 1 lease shows Anthropic is willing to take capacity from whoever can deliver, fast, regardless of competitive optics. Worth tracking which providers are named in this round's diligence calls.

Google says it caught the first AI built zero day before mass exploitation

Google Threat Intelligence Group reported on May 11 that it detected a zero day, a two factor authentication bypass for a popular open source admin platform, that was almost certainly built with an LLM. The exploit was disrupted before it could be used at scale.

▶

GTIG's tells were forensic and oddly mundane: highly annotated code, Python style docstrings inside what should have been a stealth payload, and a hallucinated CVSS score that does not exist in any database. Google explicitly says neither Gemini nor Anthropic's Mythos was the model used, which strongly implies an open weights model or a poorly guard railed third party API.

The honest framing: this is one corroborated incident, and GTIG itself notes the only reason it was caught was the model's sloppy artefacts. John Hultquist (GTIG chief analyst) implies in multiple outlets that the count of undetected AI assisted exploits is likely materially higher.

Coverage is essentially uniform across Bloomberg, CNBC, The Register, SecurityWeek, CyberScoop, The Hacker News, PYMNTS, Engadget and UPI. That kind of convergence is the strongest cross source signal of the week.

Why It Matters for CoreWeave

This is a leading indicator for two CoreWeave adjacent workloads. First, defensive AI workloads (vuln triage, red teaming, exploit detection) become a buyer category in their own right, and OpenAI just opened the GPT-5.5-Cyber trusted access program on May 15. Second, expect renewed scrutiny on which models customers run on shared infrastructure: workloads that touch security tooling will demand stronger isolation guarantees, attestation, and audit trails. Both push the conversation toward higher value, higher margin tiers of capacity rather than commodity rental.

Anthropic passes OpenAI in US enterprise adoption for the first time, Ramp index shows

Ramp's May 2026 AI Index, released May 13, has Anthropic at 34.4% enterprise adoption (up 3.8 points) versus OpenAI at 32.3% (down 2.9). It is the first crossover in the entire generative AI cycle.

▶

The dataset is Ramp's corporate card and bill pay data, so "adoption" here means paying for the service through a US business entity rather than seat counts or revenue. That distinction matters: it is a cleaner measure of business buyer preference than self reported usage, but it understates international and consumer demand. Both VentureBeat and Axios call out that caveat.

The narrative across VentureBeat, Axios, eWeek and Times Square Chronicles is that Claude Code is the wedge. A figure that keeps showing up: about 4% of all GitHub public commits worldwide are now authored by Claude Code, roughly double the prior month. I have not seen the underlying methodology, so I would treat the 4% number as approximate.

VentureBeat's important counterpoint: three things could erase this lead. Compute scarcity, token pricing pressure as competitors discount, and OpenAI's renewed enterprise push with the OpenAI Deployment Company (launched May 11). The crossover may be a peak, not a trend break.

Why It Matters for CoreWeave

Customer concentration risk and customer concentration upside are the same story here. Anthropic's surge is being fed by Claude Code, which is heavily inference dense and bursty. That is exactly the workload profile CoreWeave's reserved capacity model is built for, and it is also the profile that pressures Anthropic to find more compute, more places, more quickly. Watch for whether the next Anthropic capacity expansion lands at CoreWeave, OCI, a hyperscaler, or another neocloud; each path tells a different story about long term pricing.

IREN closes $3B convertible after Nvidia $3.4B cloud deal, pushes committed revenue past $15B

On May 15 IREN closed an upsized $3.0B convertible notes offering (initial size $2B) at a 1.00% coupon and a 32.5% conversion premium. The raise follows a five year, $3.4B managed GPU cloud contract with Nvidia and warrants potentially worth $2.1B announced May 7.

▶

IREN is now the most direct public market comparable for CoreWeave's neocloud thesis, and the market priced this deal at a premium investors do not usually give to debt heavy infrastructure. The 32.5% conversion premium and 1.00% coupon are aggressive terms for a company that was a bitcoin miner two years ago. Underwriters got there because the Nvidia anchor turns IREN into a structurally subsidised buildout: Nvidia takes warrants, IREN takes the offtake.

The follow on $625M all stock acquisition of Mirantis (reported across CoinDesk, The Block and FinanceFeeds) is the more interesting tell. It moves IREN from pure GPU rental toward a full stack AI cloud, which is the same direction CoreWeave has been pushing with managed services and now with the Rack Lifecycle Controller for Vera Rubin.

Cross source check: CoinDesk, The Block, Decrypt, CNBC, Crypto.news, FinanceFeeds and Yahoo Finance all converge on the same deal structure. The "AI deals reshape growth" framing from MEXC and Crypto.news adds a separate Microsoft contract reference that I cannot independently verify against Microsoft sources, so I would flag that piece as single sourced.

Why It Matters for CoreWeave

IREN's deal terms are the new public market benchmark for how the buy side underwrites neocloud capex. A 1.00% coupon implies investors are pricing this as quasi infrastructure with the optionality of equity upside, very different from how the same balance sheets were treated 18 months ago. That is broadly favourable for CoreWeave's cost of capital. The competitive read is sharper though: IREN now has 5 GW committed under Nvidia, plus owned generation. That is the same demand pool CoreWeave is competing for, with the same vendor co marketing the same workloads. Worth a closer look at which named accounts overlap.

The Codex moves are quieter but matter more for the agentic AI story. Codex mobile and the Windows sandbox close two of the last gaps in agentic coding workflows. Combine that with the OpenAI Deployment Company launch on May 11, and the picture is OpenAI pushing hard to recover the developer mindshare it lost to Claude Code (see story 03).

GPT-5.5-Cyber is the most under reported piece. Trusted Access for defenders is OpenAI's first explicit security vertical, and it arrived four days after Google's AI built zero day disclosure. Treat the timing as deliberate.

Why It Matters for CoreWeave

Three new sustained inference surfaces in one week: personal finance, mobile Codex, and Cyber. Each one is a long lived agent class with different latency and reliability requirements than chatbot inference. The competitive backdrop is OpenAI rebuilding enterprise pull at the exact moment Anthropic is constrained on compute. Both labs are effectively shopping for the same capacity CoreWeave sells.

Europe's AI data center economics keep slipping; CNBC frames it as a sovereignty problem

CNBC reported on May 18 that average MW priced electricity ran at $111.65 in the UK, $88.97 in Germany, $44.19 in France versus $28 in the US. Capacity costs across Frankfurt, London, Amsterdam, Paris and Dublin are forecast to rise 12% in 2026.

▶

The price spreads are not new but the framing is hardening. The piece reads less like a market analysis and more like a policy argument that Europe is structurally losing the AI race on power cost alone. France (with its nuclear baseload) is the only major European market in the same zip code as US pricing, and even there a 60% premium to US compute is a hard sell to a frontier lab.

I would caveat the specific MW prices: CNBC attributes them to IEA data, but the article does not link the underlying figures, and "average MW priced electricity" is a phrasing that conflates wholesale, retail and industrial tariffs. The directional point holds; the precise numbers may be soft.

The CNBC piece pairs with Anthropic's late April hire of a Europe data center deal lead, indicating that even with structurally worse economics, the demand to land capacity in Europe (for data residency and sovereign customer needs) is real.

Why It Matters for CoreWeave

This is the clearest macro argument for why US sited capacity continues to be the highest leverage asset class in the global AI stack. The same workloads that are demand constrained in Europe price the marginal kWh in the US at roughly a quarter of UK levels. To the extent CoreWeave's roadmap touches European expansion, the cost of capital and unit economics need to assume the 2026 to 2028 spread does not close. To the extent the roadmap stays US weighted, the Europe story is a tailwind for premium pricing on cross border workloads that route through US capacity.
